QuantFidelity — Vision
=======================

One-line pitch
--------------
QuantFidelity shows where alpha breaks between data, backtests, production, and execution.


What QuantFidelity Is
---------------------
QuantFidelity is quant strategy reliability and observability infrastructure for systematic
trading teams. It is a single reliability layer that tracks the full lifecycle of a strategy:
from raw data, through research and backtesting, into production, through execution, and out
to live P&L.

It records what a strategy was, what changed, what data it consumed, what the backtest assumed,
what was intended in production, what was actually executed, and what finally happened in live
trading. It then deterministically compares these stages and explains where the alpha leaked.

QuantFidelity is the control plane that answers one question with evidence:
"Why did this strategy stop behaving the way research said it would?"


What QuantFidelity Is NOT
-------------------------
- It is NOT a stock tracker or market dashboard.
- It is NOT a trading bot or execution engine.
- It is NOT a backtesting toy or a "build-a-strategy" sandbox.
- It is NOT a generic AI stock-picking product.
- It does NOT place orders, route trades, or take broker write actions.
- It does NOT give buy/sell/hold advice.

QuantFidelity is reliability infrastructure for systematic trading — closer to an observability
and audit platform than to anything that touches markets directly.


Why The Problem Matters
-----------------------
Quant firms spend enormous effort finding alpha. The hard, expensive, prestigious work is in
research: signal discovery, feature engineering, portfolio construction, and backtesting. But
alpha routinely breaks on the path from research to live trading, and when it breaks the cause
is rarely obvious.

A strategy that showed Sharpe 1.6 in research can deliver 0.4 live. The team is then left
asking: did the signal decay? Was the backtest overfit? Was the data revised underneath us?
Did production drift away from the research portfolio? Did risk limits block the best trades?
Was the borrow unavailable? Did execution slippage eat the edge? Did the market regime change?

Today this post-mortem is manual, slow, political, and inconclusive. It is done in notebooks,
spreadsheets, and Slack threads, weeks after capital was already lost. The cost is not just the
drawdown — it is the inability to distinguish a dead strategy from a fixable implementation
problem, which leads firms to kill good alpha and keep deploying broken alpha.


Why Quant Firms Need This Now
-----------------------------
- Strategy counts are rising. Teams run dozens to hundreds of strategies; manual forensics do
  not scale.
- Data dependencies are exploding. Alternative data, vendor revisions, and corporate actions
  create silent failure modes that backtests never see.
- The research-to-production gap is widening. Research runs in clean notebooks; production runs
  under risk limits, borrow constraints, and real execution. Nobody owns the reconciliation.
- Capital is less patient. Allocators and risk committees demand defensible explanations for
  performance, not narratives.
- The tooling does not exist. Software engineering solved this a decade ago with observability,
  error tracking, and version control. Systematic trading has no equivalent reliability layer.


Why Existing Tools Are Not Enough
---------------------------------
- Backtesting frameworks prove a strategy works in theory; they do not audit whether the
  backtest itself is believable, and they go silent the moment the strategy leaves research.
- Trade-cost analysis (TCA) tools measure execution in isolation; they do not connect slippage
  back to research assumptions or strategy changes.
- Risk systems enforce limits; they do not attribute lost alpha to the blocks they imposed.
- Data quality tools validate pipelines; they do not tie a data revision to a strategy's P&L
  swing.
- BI dashboards visualize P&L; they do not explain it.
- Generic ML/experiment trackers version models; they do not understand orders, fills, borrow,
  or market microstructure.

Each tool sees one stage. No tool sees the whole lifecycle and reconciles the stages against
each other. QuantFidelity is built specifically to be that connective, reconciling layer.


The Core Thesis: Where Alpha Breaks
-----------------------------------
Alpha breaks somewhere between data, backtests, production, and execution. The breakage falls
into a bounded set of causes:

  1. Signal decay        — the edge genuinely weakened in the market.
  2. Data issues         — bad, stale, revised, missing, or duplicated data.
  3. Backtest flaws      — lookahead, survivorship, unrealistic fills, overfitting.
  4. Production drift     — the live portfolio diverged from the research intent.
  5. Risk constraints    — risk limits blocked or resized the intended trades.
  6. Borrow unavailability — shorts could not be sourced or were recalled.
  7. Execution slippage  — fills were worse than assumed; costs higher than modeled.
  8. Market regime change — the environment the strategy was fit to no longer holds.

QuantFidelity's job is to deterministically gather evidence for each candidate cause, score it,
and tell the team which one (or which combination) most likely explains the performance change —
with the underlying evidence attached, not a guess.


The Long-Term Category: Quant Strategy Observability
----------------------------------------------------
QuantFidelity is defining a new category: Quant Strategy Observability. Just as software teams
adopted application observability and error tracking as non-optional infrastructure, systematic
trading teams will adopt strategy observability as the standard layer between research and
capital.

The category is not "analytics" and not "trading." It is reliability: continuous, evidence-based
assurance that a strategy in production still matches the strategy that was researched, and clear
attribution when it does not.


End-State Vision
----------------
In its mature form, QuantFidelity is the reliability backbone of a systematic trading firm:

- Every strategy run, backtest, dataset, intended order, fill, and P&L snapshot flows through a
  normalized event schema via a lightweight Python SDK and REST ingestion.
- Every strategy has a living lineage: what changed, when, and how performance moved afterward.
- Every backtest carries a Trust Score with the specific biases and unrealistic assumptions it
  contains.
- Every live strategy carries a Drift Score and full execution attribution: how much alpha was
  lost to risk blocks, borrow, liquidity clipping, slippage, and partial fills, versus genuine
  signal decay.
- A single Reliability Dashboard rolls these into one Overall Reliability Score per strategy,
  with a plain-language diagnosis backed by evidence.
- Firms bring their own institutional data; QuantFidelity never requires owning the market data.
- Risk and allocator teams use the same audit trail to perform manager and strategy diligence.

The end state is institutional-grade, self-hostable, and trusted enough that a risk committee
will accept a QuantFidelity diagnosis as the system of record for "what happened to this strategy."


What QuantFidelity Should Never Become
--------------------------------------
- A trading system, order router, or anything that takes broker write actions.
- An investment advisor or signal vendor that tells anyone what to buy or sell.
- A black-box "AI says your strategy is bad" oracle with no traceable evidence.
- A market-data reseller whose value depends on owning proprietary feeds.
- A hype-driven "AI-powered insights" dashboard with generic, unfalsifiable claims.
- A heavy data lake that hoards every client's raw proprietary signals and returns by default.


Non-negotiable Product Principles
---------------------------------
1. Evidence before AI.
   No diagnosis is presented without underlying, inspectable evidence. AI summarizes evidence;
   it never substitutes for it.

2. Deterministic lineage before generated explanations.
   Diffing, scoring, and lineage are computed deterministically and reproducibly first. AI
   explanation is a presentation layer on top of facts that already exist.

3. No broker write actions.
   QuantFidelity never places, modifies, routes, or cancels real orders. It observes and
   reconciles; it does not trade.

4. No investment advice.
   QuantFidelity never recommends buying, selling, or holding any instrument. It explains
   reliability, not opportunity.

5. Minimal data storage by default.
   Store metadata, summaries, hashes, and fingerprints by default. Raw data ingestion is opt-in
   and bounded. Sensitive content can be hashed or summarized rather than retained.

6. Bring-your-own-data compatible.
   The product must work with customer-provided datasets and feeds. Provider adapters are
   replaceable. Value never depends on QuantFidelity owning institutional market data.

7. Built for serious quant workflows.
   Objects, language, and UX speak the real vocabulary of systematic trading: lineage,
   assumptions, intended vs executed, implementation shortfall, borrow, slippage, regime.
   No childish SaaS framing.

8. Low-cost early architecture, institutional-grade direction.
   Start local-first and cheap (FastAPI, PostgreSQL, mock/low-cost data providers). Architect
   from day one for self-hosting, enterprise security, and institutional scale.
